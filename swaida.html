Swaida – Heart of the Hauran

Swaida city is the vibrant capital of the Swaida Governorate, situated at approximately 1,000 meters elevation on the southern slopes of Jebel al-Arab. It is the cultural, commercial, and administrative heart of the region, and the proud home of the Druze community's rich heritage in southern Syria.

The city's crown jewel is the Swaida National Museum — one of Syria's finest provincial museums. Its collections include extraordinary Roman artifacts: marble statues, bronze figurines, fine glassware, pottery, jewelry, and world-class floor mosaics from Roman villas of Shahba and surrounding sites.

Beyond the museum, Swaida offers a lively old market where traditional Druze crafts — embroidery, copperwork, and hand-painted pottery — are still made and sold. The city hosts the annual Spring Festival each April, filling its streets with music, food, and cultural performances.
