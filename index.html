History of Swaida

2,500 years of civilisation on the Basalt Mountain

Scroll to explore the timeline
Nabataean

3rd Century BC

Nabataean Settlement

The Nabataean Kingdom establishes trade routes through the Hauran. Early temples and shrines are carved into the volcanic basalt, many still visible in Kanawat today.

Roman

64 BC – 400 AD

Roman Conquest & Golden Age

Pompey incorporates the region into the Roman province of Syria. Canatha (Kanawat) joins the Decapolis. Magnificent temples, theaters, and colonnaded streets are built across the region.

Roman Imperial

244 AD

Philip the Arab Founds Philippopolis

Marcus Julius Philippus, born in Swaida, becomes Roman Emperor and founds the city of Philippopolis (Shahba) as his imperial capital — building a theater, temples, baths, and a mausoleum in under five years.

Byzantine

400 – 636 AD

Byzantine Christian Era

Roman temples are converted into Christian basilicas. The region flourishes as part of the Byzantine Empire, with remarkable churches built over earlier pagan monuments — most visibly at Kanawat's Serapeion.

Early Islamic

636 – 1100 AD

Arab-Islamic Conquest

The Battle of Yarmouk (636 AD) brings the region under Islamic rule. The Umayyad and Abbasid caliphates govern a largely peaceful Hauran, preserving most Roman-Byzantine structures intact.

Ayyubid

1174 – 1260 AD

Saladin & the Ayyubid Fortress

Saladin's Ayyubid dynasty fortifies the region against Crusader incursions. Salkhad Castle is built atop a volcanic cone — a military stronghold visible 50 km across the plain — becoming the defining landmark of the region.

Ottoman

1516 – 1918 AD

Ottoman Rule & Druze Identity

The Ottoman Empire rules for four centuries. The Druze community — arriving from Lebanon — establishes deep roots in the Jebel al-Arab, developing a distinct cultural and architectural identity using the region's black basalt stone.

Modern

1925

The Great Syrian Revolt

Swaida becomes the birthplace of the Great Syrian Revolt against French mandate rule. Sultan Pasha al-Atrash leads the uprising from the Jebel al-Arab — a defining moment in Syrian national identity.

Today

Present

Living Heritage Destination

Swaida Governorate is one of the most archaeologically rich regions in the Middle East — open-air Roman cities, Ayyubid castles, Byzantine churches, and vibrant Druze culture, all within a single day's drive.

Everything you need to know before booking your trip to Swaida.

Visa requirements depend on your nationality. Most visitors require a Syrian tourist visa, obtained through a Syrian embassy or consulate in advance. We provide a full visa guidance document upon booking and can assist with invitation letters if required. Contact us before booking and we'll advise based on your passport.

Spring (March–May) is the most beautiful season — wildflowers cover the volcanic plateau, temperatures are mild (15–25°C), and the annual Spring Festival takes place in April. Autumn (September–November) is also excellent. Summer can be hot (30–38°C) but manageable at Swaida's elevation of 1,000m. We do not recommend mid-July to mid-August for outdoor archaeological tours.

All packages include: expert local guide (English & Arabic), air-conditioned transport, entrance fees to all sites, and traditional welcome lunch. Weekend and full packages also include handpicked accommodation and all breakfasts. Flights and international transport are not included. A full itemised breakdown is provided at booking.

We keep groups small by design — a maximum of 12 people per guide. This ensures a personal, unhurried experience at every site. Private tours for individuals, couples, or families are available at a modest supplement and can be tailored to any interest (archaeology, photography, food, or culture).

Cancellations made 14+ days before departure receive a full refund. Cancellations 7–13 days prior receive a 50% refund. Cancellations within 7 days are non-refundable, but you can transfer your booking to a future date at no cost. All bookings include free date-change up to 48 hours before departure.

Swaida Governorate has remained stable and peaceful throughout recent years and is widely regarded as one of the safest regions in Syria. Our guides are local residents with deep community ties. We monitor conditions daily and will proactively contact you about any changes. Comprehensive travel insurance is included in all packages.
