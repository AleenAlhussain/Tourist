Philippopolis – City of an Emperor

Shahba is the ancient Philippopolis, founded in 244 AD by Marcus Julius Philippus — Philip the Arab — shortly after he became Roman Emperor. Philip built it near his birthplace as his imperial capital, transforming a small village into a grand Roman city in less than a decade.

Despite Philip's assassination in 249 AD, which halted construction, Shahba preserves an extraordinary Roman urban grid: a colonnaded main street, a well-preserved theater, a tetrapylon, Roman baths, temples, and the remarkable Philippeion — the mausoleum Philip built for his family.

The Shahba Museum holds a priceless collection of 3rd-century Roman mosaics — among the finest in the world — excavated from private Roman villa floors. These mosaics depict mythological scenes with extraordinary artistry and are a UNESCO-level treasure.

Roman Theater
