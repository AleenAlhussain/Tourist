Our Story

Why We Started Visit Swaida

I grew up walking among 2,000-year-old Roman temples in Kanawat, watching foreign archaeologists discover what every child in Swaida already knew — that this region is extraordinary. But almost no one outside Syria had ever heard of it.

In 2021, I launched Visit Swaida with one goal: to bring the world to our doorstep. Not as a mass-tourism destination, but as a carefully curated, deeply personal experience — guided by people who actually live here, love it, and know every hidden corner of the basalt mountain.

We don't just show you the ruins. We tell you why they matter, who built them, and what their descendants are doing today.

— Aleen Alhussain, Founder

Every tour is small-group (max 12), every guide is a local expert, and every meal is prepared by a Druze family — because the real Swaida isn't in a museum. It's at the kitchen table.

What We Stand For

The principles behind every Visit Swaida experience

Authentic

No tourist traps. Every experience is designed by people who live here and love it.

Community First

90% of every booking goes directly to local guides, restaurants, and artisans.

Sustainable

Small groups, low impact, high respect for the sites and the people who live beside them.

Educational

We believe understanding history changes how you see the present. Every tour is a lesson in humanity.
